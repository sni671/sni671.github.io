VTOL UAV

A 3D printed tricopter-style VTOL aircraft designed, built and flight tested to explore vertical takeoff, transition flight and efficient fixed-wing cruise.

Watch Flight Video

Project Overview

This project was developed as a personal UAV platform combining aircraft design, CAD modelling, aerodynamic analysis, electronics integration and real-world flight testing. The aircraft uses two front tilt motors and a rear propulsion motor, allowing it to operate in vertical flight before transitioning into forward fixed-wing flight.

Flight Video

CAD Design

The aircraft was designed in CAD around a modular 3D printed airframe, carbon fibre wing spar, carbon fibre tail boom and integrated motor mounting structure. The CAD process was used to package the tilt mechanisms, electronics, wing structure and fuselage layout before manufacturing.

Aerodynamics & CFD

CFD was used as a design aid to understand the general flow behaviour over the aircraft and identify areas of higher and lower pressure. The goal was not to perfectly model every flight condition, but to support design decisions around wing geometry, stability and aerodynamic efficiency.

Electronics & Wiring

The electronics system was built around a flight controller, ESCs, servos, GPS, pitot tube, receiver and analog video system. I have kept this section high-level to show the system architecture without turning the page into a full wiring manual.

Vertical Flight

Vertical flight testing focused on validating thrust, motor layout, stability and control authority. The front tilt motors provide lift in hover, while the control system manages attitude during takeoff, landing and low-speed operation.

Transition Flight

The transition phase is the most challenging part of the aircraft. During transition, the aircraft moves from propeller-supported hover into wing-supported forward flight. This requires careful consideration of tilt angle, airspeed, pitch control, thrust direction and control mixing.

Forward Flight

In forward flight, the aircraft behaves more like a conventional fixed-wing UAV. This mode is intended to improve efficiency compared with hover by allowing the wing to generate lift while the propulsion system provides forward thrust.

Flight Testing & Iteration

Design

Initial design decisions were made around VTOL layout, structure, manufacturability and component packaging.

Build

The airframe was manufactured using 3D printed parts combined with carbon fibre structural members.

Test

Flight testing was used to validate assumptions and identify issues that were not obvious in CAD or simulation.

Improve

The aircraft is being developed through repeated design, build, test and refinement cycles.

Key Learning

The biggest lesson from this project has been that aircraft development is an iterative process. CAD and CFD are useful tools, but real flight testing reveals integration issues, control behaviour and practical constraints that must be solved through testing and redesign.
